Roasted Peppers And Mushroom Tortilla Pizza

  • Time=Short
  • Cost=Medium
  • Cusine=Mexican

    • Ingredients:

    • Tortillas
    • Extra Virgin Olive Oil
    • Mozzarella cheese
    • Red Yellow or Green Bell Pepper (Capsicum)
    • Button mushroom
    • Kalmatta olives
    • Onions

    Instructions:

    • Turn your oven setting to broiler and keep it ready.
    • Grease your cast iron skillet with some olive oil and heat it over high heat. When it starts to shimmer, bring down the heat to medium.
    • Add olive oil and minced garlic to the marinara sauce and stir to combine. Keep aside.
    • Place one tortilla on the cast iron skillet.
    • Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce over the tortilla.
    • Spread the cheese as needed.
    • When the cheese starts to melt, add in the toppings which include onion, mushrooms, olives, and bell peppers.
    • Shift the cast iron skillet to the oven which is already under the broiler setting.
    • When you see the cheese all melted and the top starting to brown, remove and slice to serve.

    Spicy Grilled Pineapple Salsa

  • Time=Short
  • Cost=Low
  • Cusine=Mexican

    • Ingredients:

    • Extra Virgin Olive Oil
    • Pineapple
    • White Onion
    • Red Yellow and Green Bell Peppers (Capsicum)
    • Pickled Jalapenos
    • Coriander (Dhania) Leaves
    • Mint Leaves (Pudina)
    • Salt
    • Lemon juice
    • Paprika powder

    Instructions:

    • Prep all the ingredients and finely chop them.
    • Heat oil in a grill pan over medium heat.
    • Place the sliced pineapple in the pan and sprinkle with salt.
    • Cover the pan and cook the pineapple until it's soft and has a lightly grilled texture. Keep turning it around until golden brown on both sides.
    • Allow the grilled pineapple to cool and then finely chop it.
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the grilled pineapple, onions, jalapenos, bell peppers, coriander leaves, and mint leaves. Mix well.
    • Finally, add salt, lemon juice, and smoked paprika to make the Grilled Pineapple Salsa. Mix thoroughly.
    • Refrigerate the Grilled Pineapple Salsa for at least 2 hours.
    • Serve the Spicy Grilled Pineapple Salsa chilled as an appetizer alongside a meal.

    Mango Tomato Bean Salsa Recipe

  • Time=Short
  • Cost=Medium
  • Cusine=Mexican

    • Ingredients:

    • Mango (Ripe)
    • Tomato
    • Onion
    • Rajma (Large Kidney Beans)
    • Pickled Jalapenos
    • Mint Leaves (Pudina)
    • Lemon juice
    • Coriander (Dhania) Leaves
    • Salt
    • Cumin powder (Jeera)
    • Red Chilli powder

    Instructions:

    • Prep all the ingredients and keep them ready.
    • Soak the rajma for at least 8 hours.
    • Cook the rajma in the pressure cooker for about 35 minutes until soft and well cooked. Allow the pressure to release naturally.
    • Tip: It's a good idea to cook extra rajma for this salsa and keep it refrigerated for quick preparation.
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped ripe mangoes, onion, tomato, boiled rajma, jalapenos, coriander, and mint leaves.
    • Season the mango bean salsa with cumin powder, red chili powder, salt, lemon juice, and Tabasco sauce.
    • Mix the salsa well and serve chilled.
    • Serve Mango Tomato Bean Salsa as an appetizer or alongside dishes like tacos or serve it as a simple salad with other Mexican appetizers.

    Rajma Kofta In Milk And Poppy Seed Gravy

  • Time=Medium
  • Cost=High
  • Cusine=North Indian

    • Ingredients:

    • Rajma (Large Kidney Beans)
    • Cashew nuts
    • Sultana Raisins
    • Asafoetida (hing)
    • Cumin seeds (Jeera)
    • Gram flour (besan)
    • Coriander (Dhania) Leaves
    • Poppy seeds
    • Fresh cream
    • Milk
    • Water
    • Salt
    • Saffron strands

    Instructions:

    • Prepare the koftas first by soaking the rajma overnight and pressure cooking it with salt until soft.
    • Blend the boiled rajma, besan, cashew, raisins, chili powder, hing, coriander leaves, and salt in a food processor. Adjust seasoning.
    • Spread the mashed spiced rajma on a thali or a large plate. Make small round balls of rajma kofta with your palm.
    • Heat a frying pan with half ghee and fry the kofta balls for about 10 minutes. Set aside.
    • Alternatively, heat a kuzhi paniyaram pan, add a few drops of oil into each cavity, and place the balls in each cavity. Rotate them for even browning.
    • Take a kadai and heat ghee, add cumin, and allow it to sizzle. Add milk, cream, poppy seed paste, garam masala powder, and salt. Add water if needed.
    • Cook until the mixture comes to a boil, add the koftas, and switch off heat after half a minute.
    • Serve with your favorite garnish like saffron or coriander leaves.
    • Serve Rajma Kofta In Milk And Poppy Seed Gravy Recipe along with naan and rice for lunch or dinner.

    Schezwan Style Chinese Veg Samosa

  • Time=Medium
  • Cost=Medium
  • Cusine=North Indian

    • Ingredients:

    • Whole Wheat Flour
    • Sooji (Semolina/ Rava)
    • Ajwain (Carom seeds)
    • Salt
    • Extra Virgin Olive Oil
    • Carrot (Gajjar)
    • Green Bell Pepper (Capsicum)
    • Cabbage (Patta Gobi/ Muttaikose)
    • Ginger
    • Garlic
    • Green Chilli
    • Spring Onion Greens
    • Black pepper powder

    Instructions:

    • Prep all the ingredients and keep them ready.
    • For the Dough:
      • Combine all the dough ingredients in a large mixing bowl.
      • Rub the olive oil into the flour until you get coarse crumbs.
      • Add little water at a time and knead to make a firm and smooth dough.
      • Cover the dough and let it rest while preparing the filling.
    • For the Filling:
      • Heat olive oil in a wok or a heavy-bottomed pan.
      • Add ginger, garlic, green chilies, and spring onions. Stir fry until fragrant.
      • Add carrots, beans, capsicum, and cabbage. Stir fry on high heat until cooked through but firm.
      • Add cooked and chopped noodles, chili sauce, salt, and pepper. Stir until well combined. Adjust seasoning.
      • Remove from heat and let the filling cool.
    • Preheat the oil for deep frying the samosas.
    • Divide the samosa dough into 12 equal portions. Roll each portion into a small square after dusting it with flour.
    • Place a small portion of the Chinese filling into the rolled out dough.
    • Fold it like a wonton by bringing the edges together and seal the edges by pinching them well.
    • Make similar Szechuan Style Chinese Samosas with the rest of the dough and filling.
    • Deep fry the samosas until crisp and golden brown.
    • Remove from the oil and serve hot.
    • Serve the Veg Chinese Samosa as an appetizer or evening tea-time snack for parties.

    Matar Butter Masala

  • Time=Short
  • Cost=High
  • Cusine=North Indian

    • Ingredients:

    • Cashew nuts
    • Onions
    • Ginger
    • Garlic
    • Green Chilli
    • Green peas (Matar)
    • Butter (Salted)
    • Bay leaf (tej patta)
    • Turmeric powder (Haldi)
    • Kashmiri Red Chilli Powder
    • Coriander Powder (Dhania)
    • Tomatoes
    • Salt
    • Sugar
    • Fresh cream
    • Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves)
    • Coriander (Dhania) Leaves

    Instructions:

    • Boil the peas in a pressure cooker with 1/2 cup of water and some salt for one whistle. Release the pressure immediately to retain the fresh green color. Set aside.
    • Soak cashews in hot water for 15-20 minutes. Blend soaked cashews and water to make a smooth paste. Set aside.
    • In a mixer jar, combine onions, ginger, garlic, and green chili. Grind into a fine paste. Set aside.
    • Roughly chop the tomatoes and blend to make a puree.
    • Heat butter in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Add bay leaf and onion paste. Sauté for 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously.
    • Stir in salt, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and red chili powder. Sauté for 30-40 seconds.
    • Add tomato puree, cover, and cook for 5 minutes or until tomatoes are bright red and cooked completely.
    • Stir in boiled peas along with water. Adjust gravy consistency by adding more water if required.
    • Add sugar, kasuri methi, and fresh cream. Bring to a brisk boil and turn off the heat.
    • Adjust salt and seasoning, stir in freshly chopped coriander leaves.
    • Transfer Matar Butter Masala to a serving bowl and serve hot with naan and rice for a delightful meal.

    Kurkuri Bhindi And Onion Masala

  • Time=Medium
  • Cost=Medium
  • Cusine=North Indian

    • Ingredients:

    • Bhindi (Lady Finger/Okra)
    • Gram flour (besan)
    • Red Chilli powder
    • Turmeric powder (Haldi)
    • Coriander Powder (Dhania)
    • Amchur (Dry Mango Powder)
    • Salt
    • Water
    • Oil
    • Cumin seeds (Jeera)
    • Mustard seeds (Rai/ Kadugu)
    • Asafoetida (hing)
    • Green Chilli
    • Onions
    • Coriander (Dhania) Leaves

    Instructions:

    • Firstly, make the Kurkuri Bhindi:
      • Take a bowl, add sliced bhindi, besan, 1/2 teaspoon red chilli powder, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder, 1/2 teaspoon amchur powder, required salt, and a little water. Mix everything properly.
      • Heat oil in a pan and fry the coated bhindis until crispy. Remove them and place on absorbent paper to absorb extra oil. Set aside.
    • In a different wok, heat oil and add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, hing, and green chillies. Saute for 10 to 15 seconds.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute, then add chopped onions. Cook until onions become soft and translucent.
    • Add remaining red chilli powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and required salt. Mix well, cover the wok, and let it cook for 3 to 4 minutes.
    • Add the fried kurkuri bhindi and mix properly. Cook for two more minutes, then switch off the stove and garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
    • Serve Kurkuri Bhindi And Onion Masala along with roti and rice for a weekday meal.

    Kela Matar ki Sabzi

  • Time=Medium
  • Cost=Medium
  • Cusine=North Indian

    • Ingredients:

    • Raw Banana
    • Green Peas (Matar)
    • Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
    • Green Chilli
    • Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
    • Coriander Powder (Dhania)
    • Cumin Powder (Jeera)
    • Amchur (Dry Mango Powder)
    • Lemon
    • Oil
    • Coriander (Dhania) Leaves
    • Salt

    Instructions:

    • Cut the raw bananas into half and pressure cook them with 1/4 cup of water for 2 to 3 whistles. Allow the pressure to release naturally.
    • Peel the skin from the cooked raw banana and slice them into roundels.
    • Heat oil in a kadai over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and green chilies. When they sizzle, add salt, coriander powder, cumin powder, amchur powder, garam masala powder, and turmeric powder. Mix well.
    • Add boiled peas and cooked raw banana slices. Mix well.
    • Sprinkle some water, cover, and cook the Kela Matar ki Sabzi until all the masalas coat well (for about 4 to 5 minutes).
    • Check the salt and spices, adjust to taste. Add lemon juice and chopped coriander leaves. Stir well.
    • Serve the Kela Matar ki Sabzi with roti, paratha, salad, and steamed rice.
    • This diabetic-friendly dish can be served along with yogurt, salad, and buttermilk for a balanced meal.

    Thakkali Gotsu

  • Time=Short
  • Cost=High
  • Cusine=South Indian

    • Ingredients:

    • Sesame (Gingelly) Oil
    • Mustard seeds (Rai/ Kadugu)
    • Curry leaves
    • Garlic
    • Pearl onions (Sambar Onions)
    • Tomatoes
    • Tamarind
    • Turmeric powder (Haldi)
    • Salt
    • Jaggery

    Instructions:

    • Prepare all the ingredients and keep them ready.
    • Soak the tamarind in hot water for 20 minutes. Extract about 1/2 cup of thick tamarind water.
    • Roughly chop the tomatoes and pulse them in a mixer grinder for a chunky sauce, not a fine puree.
    • Heat oil in a pressure cooker over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and allow them to crackle. Add curry leaves, garlic, and onions. Sauté until onions turn tender and translucent.
    • Add crushed tomatoes, tamarind water, turmeric powder, sambar powder, salt, and jaggery. Stir and pressure cook for 2 whistles.
    • Turn off the heat and allow the pressure to release naturally. Open the cooker and give the Tomato Gotsu a stir.
    • Check the salt and spices, adjust to taste. Transfer Tomato Gotsu to a serving bowl and serve hot.
    • Serve Tomato Gotsu with dosa and chutney for breakfast.
